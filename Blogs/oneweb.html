The uses of Metadata - what, why and how

Metadata is any auxiliary important information about a webpage that is not displayed on the webpage but is used by search engines and browsers to determine how the webpage appears in search results and how the data of the webpage is displayed. Examples of metadata include; the webpage’s author, when last the page was modified or even a brief description of what the page contains.

Reflection on, a brief history of Web Robots

The World Wide Web is an immense source of knowledge with almost infinite different webpages. It would be impossible for any human, or even group of humans to transverse it all and analyse every single page but somehow search engines are still able to provide you with links to website containing exactly the key words and information you are looking for, how is this possible? Introducing Web Robots, small software programs that have been coded to move from one webpage to another as they perform an array of pre-programmed tasks. Not only do these Web Robots (or “Bots”) save an unimaginable amount of time, they are also less likely to make mistakes than a human completing the same tasks would be. These Bots do however pose their own unique threats. Just as it is possible to program them to complete some useful task it is also possible to program malicious Bots that scour the web testing webpages security and try to cause havoc. Whilst Bots have a number of both pros and cons I think it is important as web developers to take both of these into account and follow best practices to ensure we create web pages that make the most of Bots functionality without becoming vulnerable to the potential damage that can be cause by malicious bots.

The importance of Accessibility

As a society, the internet has become an integral part of our lives and has completely transformed the way we do things. From the way we communicate, the way we learn and even the way we shop. As web developers it important to remember that some users of the internet may have disabilities and that these disabilities should be taken into account when creating webpages. Webpages should allow for these people to interact with the site as easily as any other person would. The importance of ensuring accessibility for all lies in the core motivation for the creation of the World Wide Web as a platform that is free for any and all to use. By ensuring webpages are usable by all, we ensure that as many people are included in the Web experience as possible.

Accessibility tools

There are a wide variety of accessibility tools that have already been developed, from programs that will read out the content of a webpage for visually impaired users, to assistive technologies that can be linked to a computer and allow physically disabled users to engage with the internet without the use of a traditional keyboard and mouse. As developers, it is our responsibility to ensure that we take every possible measure to ensure the web pages we create are accessible to all. This includes everything from our choice of text style and size, to ensuring we include the necessary information so that a screen reader can explain the site in the best way possible. Other useful tools include; ensuring that forms have rigorous exception handling capabilities and that the information that needs to be filled in has an accurate description, ensuring motion is minimized and including that there is an option to switch animations on a page off. By taking the necessary precautions I believe webpages can be created for all people to enjoy regardless of who they are or what disability they have.

How does the Medium is the Message impact our understanding of the web?

As explored in last week’s blog post, the philosophy behind the Medium is the Message is essentially the fact that the medium through which a message is delivered can have a more profound effect on us than the message itself. This ideology impacts our understanding of the web because it allows us to explore how the existence of the web has affected us as individuals as well as the way that we think. Our thought process has become one that resembles the web, just as we move from one page to another through hyperlinks our brains have been programmed to think in a pragmatical interconnected way in which our ability to associate related material has become pronounced. We have also developed into being able to absorb and process information a lot faster because it is presented to us quickly and continuously through RSS feeds and various forms of social media. It is important to note these developmental changes and identify that at their root they were motivated by the medium of the Web rather than the content on it.
